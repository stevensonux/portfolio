Logo and Branding Development

2021, Staff Finder Inc. Michigan

Project Overview

At a Glance...

  • Project: Design an effective, aesthetic, and memorable brand for the company.
  • Goals: Ensure that the new design communicates the company's intention, as well as provides an updated appearance.
  • Target Audience: Potential clients, prospective hires of the company
  • Role: Volunteer designer

Introduction

     This job came to me from a close friend in need of redesign options for her company, Staff Finders Inc., a manufacturing recruitment agency out of mid michigan. She emphasized particularly that the previous branding had gone out of date and required a more up-to-date look to draw in potential clients and recruits. After a meeting and a couple cups of coffee, we discussed what her expectations out of a perfect redesign might look like to her; something modern and sleek that reflected the company values, as well as a refined and professional design that gave the company strong visual credibility. All things I could do!

THE PROCESS

Mood Boarding, Sketching, Mockups, Adobe Illustrator

Getting Started

     After speaking with my friend, the client, about her company, its values, history, and what she wanted out of the branding, I came to narrow out a few key words throughout our conversation. These key words would define the rest of the design process, and be adjectives to remember throughout design decisions:

  • Modern
  • Sleek
  • Professional
  • Crisp

     From these concepts, I could begin gathering ideas and inspiration from outside sources. I got to work, figuring out a design that would better define the company's intent while still maintain some of their established branding.


    First, I analyzed the initial design: An idea on movement, the idea of exchange with the directions of the arrows, and a slanted font to further emphasize that movement. This idea of movement in design is a great one for a staffing agency, though I was worried that the initial design conveyed almost *too* much of it, resulting in ideas of being rushed or hurried. I decided to take the idea and dial it back while maintaining the integrity of the original concept.

Mood Boarding

     For a task like this one, I felt a mood board to establish a closer idea of the overall revised concept. I took particular interest and inspiration in incorporating a personal favorite concept of biomimicry and the influence of nature; what is more hard working, fast travelling, and strong in connection than the honeybee? The honeycomb shapes allow the use of rigid lines in a notable pattern that ties with the concept.

Mockup Sketching

     Once I had my idea, I began developing some low-fidelity mockup sketches. I sent these to my client along with a proposal for the rebrand and got her feedback on the concept before revisions.

First Prototypes

          After discussing the initial mockup concepts with my client, I had her choose a favorite and got to work with designing the first prototype in Adobe Illustrator.

Final Branding Examples

     With the finalized product for the logo, I moved on toward the concept of branding, creating a few examples of what their image could look like for the final presentation to the client. After this, I presented the client with the finished result; and she loved it!
