Intranet Development 

2019, Real Estate One IT, Southfield MI

Project Overview

At a Glance...

  • Project: Intranet service development across multiple branches 
  • Goals: Facilitate managing employees and communication between each of the 5 branches within the Real Estate One company
  • Target Audience: Managers and Employees
  • The Challenge: Building a system that accomplished specific tasks for each branch, while also encouraging and facilitating company interaction between branches through shared intranet features       
  • Role: Lead, under supervision.

Introduction

     I first began this job as an intern newly introduced into the world of User Experience Design, and to date it had been one of my biggest and most fulfilling challenges that not only gave real world job experience, but honed my skills in so many areas of product and UX design

The ultimate goal was to create a pleasant, easy to use, and effective intranet service that met the wants, needs, and aesthetics of 5 different company branches, while simultaneously encouraging and facilitating communication between these branches and their employees. THE PROCESS

Project Management, User Research, HTML, CSS, JS, Claromentis

Getting Started

     In the beginning, I was paired with the IT department's lead and the company web designer as one of the primary guides and leads for the project. The intranet service would be hosted through UK platform "Claromentis," which we would be trained on for a total of 7 zoom-oriented training sessions with Claromentis company support.

Interviews

     With a project of this size, there were a lot of users' needs we needed to take into consideration in order to create the perfect service. I ended up setting interviews with representatives from each branch, ranging from department or branch leads to branch agents and employees with strong ideas for the finished product.
Before the interviews started, I compiled a list of some of the most important questions I had for the interviewees. I started off asking them to walk me through a day in their shoes. Who did they need to talk to? When would they need to talk to them? What tasks were tedious due to poor communication? What would a perfect work day look like?
After asking some of these lighter questions, I pressed the interviews further into the technical side. Did the company already have an intranet service? What was it like? Were there things they liked about it, or didn't like? If there wasn't an intranet service, do they have needs they think could be met by one? What was one thing they liked about their current workflow that they'd like to see carried over? What about something they disliked, and would like to live without?
Using these questions and others, I conducted 20 minute interviews with over 20 individuals from different branches within the company. I was able to then sort and analyze the results of each and create a general idea of what the recurring themes of each branch's needs consisted of.
